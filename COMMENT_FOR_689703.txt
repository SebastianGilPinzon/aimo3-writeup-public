The writeup is a falsification study documenting 16 controlled modifications to the AIMO3 public-consensus inference pipeline on gpt-oss-120b-MXFP4 + vLLM 0.11.2. The central claim is that, within the design space of our 16 tested inference-only departures, no modification exceeded the baseline — the best-performing configuration on this substrate is the unmodified public-consensus recipe, and our final submission is byte-identical to it. Public = private = 42/50 provides a clean end-to-end reproducibility signal.

Three substrate-level traps are documented in §6 with runnable pytest-based detectors (CPU-runnable, <1 s): (a) LoRA-on-MXFP4 merge-then-requantize collapse (v13 regression −2 on LB), (b) EAGLE-3 draft-target speculative decoding producing zero output tokens on vLLM 0.11.2 + MXFP4 + MoE (v15 regression −42 on LB), and (c) sqrt-prior Bayesian vote re-weighting systematically inverting correct majorities via anti-Condorcet conditions (v18 regression −19 on LB). Each reduces to a known general mechanism (QA-LoRA / EAGLE-3 feature alignment / Condorcet Jury Theorem) and has a specific AIMO3-substrate manifestation.

The Colab notebook is fully Kaggle-agnostic: installs vLLM 0.11.2 + openai_harmony from PyPI, downloads gpt-oss-120b from HuggingFace, and runs the full pipeline (Harmony prompt + K=8 parallel rollouts + TIR via stateful Jupyter kernel + entropy-weighted majority voting + early-stop) against the 10 reference problems. The two intentional differences from the Kaggle submission are documented in the notebook (gpu_memory_utilization 0.94→0.90, PYTORCH_ALLOC_CONF — Colab headroom only). All 4 figures in the writeup are regenerated by the included `figures/generate_figures.py` script.
